Dark Matter

A hypothetical form of matter whose evidence for existence can be seen across many scales in the universe. For nearly 100 years its nature has remained a mystery.

Non-luminus

It does not emmit or absorb light. Its presence was deduced purely due to its gravitational effects

Abundant & heavy

Forms structures within which galaxies and clusters form. There’s 5x more dark matter than conventional matter in the universe

Stable & Elusive

A stable and neutral form of matter that interacts weakly with itself and others

New particle

No fundamental particle from the Standard Model behaves this way

Directly Detecting Dark Matter

Accellerators attempt to produce Dark Matter, other Observatories look for Dark Matter disintegration. We want to directly observe the interaction of local Dark Matter with our detector.

Read more about our idea

Xenon Observatory

With a 40-100 tonne Xenon TPC we could achieve a lot more.

Xenon has proven to be an excellent target for both simplistic and complex datk matter particle interactions. In addition to datk matter detection, at natural abundance Xenon provides unique avenues for furthering our understanding of the nature of neutrinos. Furthermore, it is capable of precicely measuring neutrino interactions at unparalleled threshold and precision.

Vanilla Dark Matter

Excellent sensitivity to Spin Independent and Spin-Dependent interactions for GeV and sub-GeV Weakly Interacting Massive Particles

Exotic Dark Matter

Sensitive to a plethora of exotic particles due to excellent energy threshold and resolution; axion-like particles, dark photons, composite, mirror, assymetric models etc.

Neutrino Physics

Sensitive to neutrinoless double beta decay of Xe-136 and double electron capture of Xe-124. Also sensitive to neutrino magnetic moment

Solar Physics

Able to constrain solar metallicity models through measurements of pp Be-7 and B-8 neutrinos

Cosmic Rays

Sensitive to atmospheric neutrino measurements at lower energies previously incredibly challenging

Supernova

An excellent early alert device for a supernova event for multi-messanger astrophysics
